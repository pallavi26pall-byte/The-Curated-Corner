30 Dreamy Nail Designs You Need to Try This Season

Pallavi K.
Beauty & Lifestyle Editor
June 20, 2026 8 min read 📌 2,400 saves

Love these nail ideas? Save this post to your Pinterest board so you never lose your inspo! 💅

If your nail appointments are starting to feel a little same-old, same-old, you're not alone. We've been deep in the Pinterest rabbit hole so you don't have to be — and these are the nail designs that are lighting up everyone's saved boards this season.

Whether you're a gel girlie, a press-on queen, or you do your own nails at home, there's something in this list for every skill level. Bookmark this page, because you're going to want to come back to it before your next appointment.

🌸 1. Glazed Donut Nails

Hailey Bieber truly changed the game with this one and we're not over it. The glazed donut effect — that sheer, pearlescent, almost-wet finish — is still one of the most-pinned nail aesthetics of the year. The secret? A chrome powder applied over a sheer pink or nude base.

✨ Pinterest Tip Search "glazed donut nails chrome" on Pinterest for hundreds of variations — from icy blue to warm rosé gold. The boards are *chef's kiss*.

🌺 2. Floral Nail Art

Flowers on nails are having a major moment — and not in a grandma way. Think ultra-delicate micro-florals, 3D gel flowers, and watercolor petals on a clean neutral base. The key is keeping the base simple so the florals can shine.

  • Micro florals — tiny flowers using a thin nail art brush or a dotting tool
  • 3D gel flowers — built up with clear builder gel for a raised, sculptural look
  • Pressed flower nails — actual dried flowers sealed under gel top coat
  • Watercolor blooms — thin, watery polish layered for a delicate painted effect
"Your nails are always done before you walk out the door — they're the finishing touch that tells the world you pay attention to details."

🤍 3. Quiet Luxury Nails

The quiet luxury aesthetic isn't just for fashion — it's taken over the nail world too. Think understated elegance: sheer nudes, soft creams, barely-there pinks, and the occasional thin gold line as a single accent. Less is truly more here.

The shapes trending with quiet luxury nails are almond and soft square — both polished and timeless. Avoid anything too sharp or overly dramatic; the whole vibe is effortless refinement.

💡 Pro Tip For the most flattering quiet luxury look, choose a nude that's 1-2 shades lighter than your natural skin tone. It elongates fingers and looks expensive without trying.

🎀 4. Bow & Ribbon Details

The coquette aesthetic is everywhere and nail art is no exception. Tiny bows — either painted on or as nail charms — are the perfect detail for a feminine, doll-like manicure. Layer them with a sheer pink base for maximum girly effect.

✨ 5. Abstract & Negative Space

If you love a modern art moment on your fingertips, abstract nails are your calling. Swirled lines, geometric shapes, and bold color blocking all look incredible — especially when paired with negative space (leaving part of the natural nail bare as a design element).

🌙 More Designs to Try

  • French tips with a twist (coloured, wavy, or extra thick)
  • Marble effect nails in soft grey and white
  • Ombre fade from blush to white
  • Gold foil accents over matte nudes
  • Swirled pastel clouds
  • Tortoiseshell in warm brown and amber
  • Starry night tips in navy and silver
  • Cherry print on white (the most viral of the season!)
  • Mermaid scales in iridescent teal
  • Linen textured nails
📌 Save to Pinterest! Each of these designs has hundreds of pins on Pinterest. Search the name + "nails tutorial" or "nails inspo" to find the exact look that calls to you.

Tools You'll Need at Home

Want to try these at home? Here's what every nail art lover needs in their kit:

  • Thin nail art brush (size 0 or 00 for detail work)
  • Dotting tool set (double-ended is ideal)
  • Chrome powder + applicator sponge
  • Gel top coat (cures under UV lamp)
  • UV/LED nail lamp
  • Nail foil strips
  • Cuticle oil (the unsung hero of gorgeous nails)
